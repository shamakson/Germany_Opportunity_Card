# Germany Opportunity Card: Skilled Worker & Point-Based Pathways

[![License: CC BY 4.0](https://img.shields.io/badge/License-CC%20BY%204.0-lightgrey.svg)](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/)

This repository contains presentation materials on the **Germany Opportunity Card (Chancenkarte)**, with a focus on the **recognized skilled-worker route** and the **point-based route** for qualified non-EU jobseekers.

The slides were prepared for an informational session at **RCCG Dominion Parish, Bremen**.

---

## Overview

Germany introduced the Opportunity Card as part of its wider skilled immigration reforms. The card allows qualified non-EU nationals to enter Germany without a prior job offer and search for suitable employment locally.

The presentation explains:

- Why Germany introduced the Opportunity Card
- The difference between the recognized skilled-worker route and the point-based route
- The baseline requirements for the point-based route
- How the 6-point system works
- Examples of successful scoring profiles
- Required application documents
- Proof of funds and financial preparation
- Motivation letter expectations
- Nigeria-specific application considerations
- What the Opportunity Card gives applicants — and what it does not guarantee
- What happens after arrival in Germany

---

## Presentation Scope

This presentation is designed as an educational guide for prospective applicants, community groups, and career-mobility discussions.

It is especially useful for applicants who want to understand:

1. Whether they may qualify through the **recognized skilled-worker route**
2. Whether they may qualify through the **point-based route**
3. How to calculate their possible points
4. What documents they should begin preparing
5. How to avoid common misunderstandings about the Opportunity Card

## Key Topics Covered

### 1. Background

The slides provide context on Germany’s skilled-labour shortage and explain how the Opportunity Card fits into Germany’s Skilled Immigration Act reforms.

### 2. Eligibility Pathways

The presentation distinguishes between two main pathways:

#### Pathway 1: Recognized Skilled Worker

Applicants whose academic or vocational qualification is fully recognized as equivalent to a German qualification may qualify through the skilled-worker route.

In this route, the points system is generally not required, although proof of financial means still applies.

#### Pathway 2: Point-Based Route

Applicants whose qualification is not fully recognized in Germany may apply through the point-based route.

This route generally requires:

- An eligible academic or vocational qualification
- Minimum language proof: German A1 or English B2
- Proof of sufficient financial means
- At least 6 points under the selection criteria

---

## Point-Based Route Summary

Applicants need at least **6 points**.

| Criterion | Points |
|---|---:|
| Partial recognition of qualification | 4 |
| Shortage occupation | 1 |
| Professional experience | 2–3 |
| German language skills | 1–3 |
| English C1+ or native English speaker | 1 |
| Age | 1–2 |
| Previous legal stay in Germany | 1 |
| Spouse/life partner also eligible | 1 |

---

## Important Clarifications

The Opportunity Card may allow applicants to:

- Enter Germany without a prior job offer
- Search for qualified employment locally
- Work part-time while job searching
- Do short trial work linked to the job-search goal
- Transition to a longer-term residence title after securing a suitable offer

However, it does **not** guarantee:

- A job offer
- Unlimited work rights
- Automatic permanent residence
- Automatic citizenship
- Automatic family reunification
- Approval without complete documents
